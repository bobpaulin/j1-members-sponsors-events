Don't get discouraged

Ask for help from local Universities

Getting Sponsors

AKA living on other people's money

Add notes

There is value in Users Group

  • Where professionals that care for their career show up
  • Either looking to learn and network, or pizza

Letting sponsors free access to your user group is not healthy

  • Members get turned-off if it's another Sales pitch and employment venue
  • Sponsors then don't want to help because they don't have neither candidates and havent paid before

Lock-down your member access

  • Only you can access their communications
  • Don't advertise a sponsor just because they want you to

Once you have created a group that has value for your members

  • You can start asking for "help" from sponsors as they want access to your members

Members tolerate sponsors if:

  • Better speakers
  • More sessions
  • More topics
  • More services
  • Pizza
  • As long as Sponsors are not the focus

Understanding Sponsors

Add notes

What do sponsors want from your JUG?

  • Access to top talent
  • Get the word out
  • Networking

What can your JUG offer?

  • Email Promotions
  • Ad space
  • A captive audience

Capitalize on your group value to get sponsors

The Easy Route

  • Ask for Space sponsor
  • Food sponsor
  • Free Giveaway sponsor

The Business Route

  • Create Sponsorship levels

    We have bronze, silver, gold, platinum With different perks and access

Where do i find them?

  • Your Employer
  • At your meetings. If you build it they will come
  • Recruiting companies
  • Technology companies (anything that's big in Java)
  • Educational companies
  • Java developer products (intellij, JRebel, Birt, etc)
Often the Best Sponsors are the "new" guys in town. They sponsor because they want people to know they're there.

Getting Speakers

How not to be the featured presenter every week

Think big...

Don't be afraid to reach out to BIG names

Attend relevant conferences in your area and engage

Timing is everything

Look for big conferences coming to your area. Generally promoters will send top quality speakers to promote them

Most conference organizers love to work with the local JUG (NFSF, ACM)

Become a part of the JCP (as a member you get access to speakers)

Lurk in the Java Users Group Leaders List

Getting a Venue

  • Universities, Colleges, Local government will help
  • Local companies and/or sponsors
  • Rent a space (~ 500$ for a room holding 50 ~ 80 people)

Be Serial

  • If working with speakers, let them know that dates are changing until they confirm
  • And that dates could still change because of the venue
  • When looking for a venue,
  • Don't talk to possible venue providers at once.
  • Worse, trying to cancel a booked venue (alienate sponsors)

Use Free Tools

  • Trello
  • Google Docs

Getting Attendees

  • Reach out to your member base as discussed in the members section
  • Be excited (they are)
  • Ask for help spreading the word

What about food?

  • Use your leverage (100 people coming, you can discount pizza pies)
  • Or get a food sponsor

What about no-shows

- http://www.javapubhouse.com/2013/06/jug-post-ok-i-have-meeting-and-am.html

  • Expect 20% no-shows
  • Don't announce event too early
  • Keep reminding
  • If the event is Full, ask for "Help"
  • Overbook (if capacity is 100, book for 120)
  • Have cool giveaways (provided by sponsors for example)
  • Charge a fee (If you really need to)
